Comprehensive assessment rubrics

Question Type Category Maximum Marks Mark Distribution Examples
Objective Questions
Multiple Choice (MCQ) Knowledge-Based 1 mark each Wrong spelling/scratched answers: 0 mark What color is the sky on a clear day?
True/False Knowledge-Based 1 mark each No partial marks, must be completely correct The Earth is the largest planet in our solar system. (True/False)
Fill-in-the-blank Knowledge-Based 1 mark each Answer must be exact with correct spelling The process of plants making their food is called __________.
Matching Questions Comprehension-Based Varies (usually 1 mark per correct match) All matches must be correct to receive full marks Match the countries with their capitals
Subjective Questions
Short Answer Comprehension-Based 2 marks each Main idea/concept/definition: 1.5 marks, Spelling: 0.5 marks Why do plants need sunlight to grow?
Paraphrasing Exercises Comprehension-Based 3 marks each Accurate meaning: 1.5 marks, Vocabulary: 1 mark, Grammar: 0.5 marks Paraphrase the given sentence
Application-Based Questions
Scenario-based MCQs Application-Based 2 marks each Correct answer: 1.5 marks, Appropriate justification: 0.5 marks John has a monthly income of $3000. Calculate his savings.
Problem-solving Exercises Application-Based 5 marks each Correct approach: 2 marks, Calculation steps: 2 marks, Final answer: 1 mark Calculate the area of a triangle with base 10cm and height 5cm.
Case Studies Application-Based 10 marks each Identification of issues: 3 marks, Analysis: 4 marks, Recommendations: 2 marks, Presentation: 1 mark Analyze the business case study and recommend strategies
Data Interpretation Analytical/Reasoning 7 marks each Data extraction: 2 marks, Analysis: 3 marks, Conclusions: 2 marks Interpret the sales data chart and identify trends
Critical Thinking Questions
Critical Thinking Scenarios Analytical/Reasoning 8 marks each Issue identification: 2 marks, Analysis: 3 marks, Evaluation: 2 marks, Presentation: 1 mark A car has run out of gas. What could be the problem?
Argumentative Tasks Evaluation and Synthesis 10 marks each Thesis statement: 2 marks, Supporting arguments: 4 marks, Counterarguments: 2 marks, Structure: 2 marks Argue for or against the implementation of school uniforms
Project-based Assessments Evaluation and Synthesis 20 marks each Research: 5 marks, Content: 8 marks, Creativity: 4 marks, Presentation: 3 marks Draw a picture of a house using only shapes
